Personality Compare and Contrast

Green: Assigned Personality
Orange: Challenging opposites

Challenging opposites are types of personalities with the most potential for personality clash and conflict. However these conflicting personality types also offer the most room for growth. Since those involved in a challenging opposite relationship tend to be the most different, one person’s strengths are another’s weakness and so on.

Our groups challenging opposites are Rodrigo and Cameron Cameron, Andrew and Rodrigo Daniel, Andrew, and Rodrigo

These 3 challenging personality types are the Mediator Introverted, Intuitive, Perfectionistic, Diplomat Campaigner Extroverted, Openhearted, Open Minded, Creative and Perceptive Virtuoso Introverted, Observant, Thoughtful and Turbulent

In the group, the following members appear to be the most distinctly different in approach and extroverted/ introvertedness according to the Myer Briggs 16 Personalities test. However there are still some very strong similarities across all three challenging opposites. They all mention the idea of being Perceptive, Intuitive or Observant which are all very similar attributes as opposed to say one personality being described as inattentive.



How We Learn

From the test results, a large proportion of our group are visual learners, I.e they learn by reading or seeing pictures. This means that statistically, the majority of our group will struggle with spoken direction as they are easily distracted by noise.

Considering this, it would be beneficial to meet in quiet places, not in libraries or cafeterias, and to either use a white board, share someone's screen if meeting online, or by turning on all video cameras to benefit the majority of the group.

Also creating a meeting notes list at the end so that the group may refer to it later would be ideal.
